# 📘 Chapter 6.4 — Multimodal NLP Systems

---

So far, we have focused mainly on **text-based NLP**.

👉 But in real life, information comes in many forms:

* Text
* Images
* Audio
* Video

👉 Modern AI systems combine these together:

# 🔹 Multimodal NLP Systems

---

## 🔹 What is Multimodal NLP?

👉 Multimodal NLP refers to systems that can:

* Understand **multiple types of data (modalities)**
* Combine them to perform tasks

---

### 🔹 Example

Input:

* Image of a dog 🐕
* Text: “What is in this image?”

👉 Output:

> “A dog is playing in the park”

---

## 🔹 What are Modalities?

👉 Modalities = different types of input data

| Modality | Example   |
| -------- | --------- |
| Text     | Sentences |
| Image    | Photos    |
| Audio    | Speech    |
| Video    | Clips     |

---

## 🔹 Why Multimodal Systems are Needed

Single modality is often not enough.

---

### Example:

Text:

> “He is running”

👉 Missing context:

* Who is “he”?
* Where is he running?

---

👉 With image:

* We can see the person, place, activity

👉 Better understanding ✅

---

## 🔹 Real-Life Analogy

Think of humans:

* We see (vision)
* We hear (audio)
* We read (text)

👉 We combine all to understand the world

👉 Multimodal AI works the same way

---

## 🔹 Architecture of Multimodal Systems

---

## 🔸 Step 1: Input Processing

* Text → tokenization
* Image → feature extraction
* Audio → waveform processing

---

## 🔸 Step 2: Encoding

👉 Convert each modality into embeddings

---

## 🔸 Step 3: Fusion

👉 Combine information from different modalities

---

## 🔸 Step 4: Output Generation

👉 Produce final result
