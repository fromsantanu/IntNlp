# 📘 Chapter 1.5 — Simple NLP Tasks

---

Now that we understand how text is processed and how language works, let’s look at **basic NLP tasks**.

👉 These are the **first practical problems** NLP tries to solve.
👉 They are simple but form the **foundation of advanced systems** like chatbots and translation tools.

---

## 🔹 What are NLP Tasks?

An NLP task is a **specific job** we want a computer to perform on text.

👉 Example:

* Identify important words
* Detect sentiment
* Classify text into categories

Think of it like giving small exercises to a student before solving big problems.

---

## 🔹 1. Tokenization

Tokenization is the process of **splitting text into smaller parts (tokens)**.

![Image](https://images.openai.com/static-rsc-4/qf9RtawlhvYMakNvfNwbo-Vno45zOmlEGp5N2v5C0J_jLC-43Gx3LW6NCB5gCbwBfueRy3TPTm4UAYIPSpwoTrkffphQJVxWbe3yGEVc4phnCCIQzLGJ9uQBPVNcplMrfKx4fj_IJ3jKEXbZ1DFBHNwzwdFI-mYs0vuazUN_R4ZxvJ0Ko2bGd7NpjSK8QoPW?purpose=fullsize)

### Example:

* Input: “I love NLP”
* Output: [“I”, “love”, “NLP”]

👉 Why important?

* It is the **first step** in almost every NLP task

---

## 🔹 2. Part-of-Speech (POS) Tagging

This task assigns **grammatical roles** to words.

![Image](https://images.openai.com/static-rsc-4/fNcpoSzrIrjCGAyGfRlWuOjeLeSoC9P_FvhfNO3MCwEi_buJ8YVZy3RNgtB3uTCOo1YKJ7lwroZDzeSULH6KDj_tGtPB2PNxhGrgP4mrYUw2wxnDQoesf9IZ5ENCG8ls7cqEKhdG2HidTiM-_tRSWw2qZQXfBHqe4oHtJrHu_Tq_EF_cnl05BX_mAGWB71VR?purpose=fullsize)

### Example:

* “She eats apples”

| Word   | POS Tag |
| ------ | ------- |
| She    | Pronoun |
| eats   | Verb    |
| apples | Noun    |

👉 Why important?

* Helps understand **sentence structure**

---

## 🔹 3. Named Entity Recognition (NER)

NER identifies **important names or entities** in text.

![Image](https://images.openai.com/static-rsc-4/zENUBiTlzyVbfujG8qBOSGaGI4OTwgDysK3NrM0_BUtjzj2FSGwFskGy7PAXUJ4pmJdYPJ9KBdLa0ZquifoO_AOB9l3xOTLvbG8sIE-udryT0lGc8_bB2w33X4ULOILQwqZDAQMgN3WNUjzkXTQgB_OmHeFbkfqh9rn2r-gA3-Gc249SjZKdHcRUPiZUwQ6b?purpose=fullsize)

### Example:

* “Ravi lives in Kolkata”

👉 Output:

* Ravi → Person
* Kolkata → Location

👉 Why important?

* Used in search engines, information extraction, etc.

---

## 🔹 4. Text Classification

Text classification means **assigning a category to text**.

![Image](https://images.openai.com/static-rsc-4/UAgjAuWI2WCqS8adzTRkoOezSJlL8oFjjjCHhnIaIZRvjIoY9nnSs2Fyxi12VmcKOp1gQP5eWbUWMpc5udDQSVtAfPJepgJqrCjB7XVyXAuFjZrE6_fBVbKI2PSMobxszMqcECy5oUrx-qlcutl44DIW-9QcLQoXwtv3doSOlaNZTlSLncAJzojqo2pjatfP?purpose=fullsize)

### Example:

* Email → Spam or Not Spam
* News → Sports / Politics / Business

👉 Why important?

* Widely used in filtering and recommendation systems

---

## 🔹 5. Sentiment Analysis

This task detects **emotion or opinion** in text.

![Image](https://images.openai.com/static-rsc-4/Fgx3-JghOo7xVGn792saBn7kj4GgGMUSAPfLacBAZueroBNjB4XD7KGMhwGCC69WMMeReWSiajZNzzl_l7RIqPnAKP4YJp_3ndPBUbujy0a3xS5RFMMSGAX-keRkRnoksjaDpL__NRK9kYPilENOhNqfUsXWPPxKUbgdGuAcFdXn40Rk9XDBlAl7cibGwrZt?purpose=fullsize)

### Example:

* “This movie is amazing!” → Positive 😊
* “This is terrible” → Negative 😞

👉 Why important?

* Used in product reviews, social media analysis

---

## 🔹 6. Stemming and Lemmatization

These tasks reduce words to their **base form**.

### Example:

* “running”, “runs” → “run”

👉 Why important?

* Helps group similar words together
* Improves model performance

---

## 🔹 7. Text Similarity

Measures how similar two sentences are.

### Example:

* “I like apples”
* “I love apples”

👉 High similarity

👉 Why important?

* Used in search engines and recommendation systems

---

## 🔹 8. Keyword Extraction

Extracts **important words** from text.

### Example:

* Input: “NLP is used in machine learning and AI”
* Output: “NLP”, “machine learning”, “AI”

👉 Why important?

* Helps in summarization and indexing

---

## 🔹 9. Language Detection

Identifies the language of a text.

### Example:

* “I love India” → English
* “আমি ভালো আছি” → Bengali

👉 Why important?

* Used in multilingual systems

---

## 🔹 10. Text Summarization (Basic Level)

Creates a **short version of text**.

### Example:

* Input: Long article
* Output: Short summary

👉 Why important?

* Saves time and improves readability

---

## 🔹 How These Tasks Fit Together

Let’s take a simple sentence:

> “Ravi loves cricket and lives in Kolkata”

### NLP pipeline:

1. Tokenization → split words
2. POS tagging → identify roles
3. NER → Ravi (Person), Kolkata (Location)
4. Classification → Sports-related text
5. Sentiment → Positive

👉 Multiple tasks work together to understand the text.

---

## 🔹 A Simple Real-Life Analogy

Think of a teacher checking a sentence:

* Break into words → Tokenization
* Identify grammar → POS tagging
* Recognize names → NER
* Understand meaning → Sentiment

👉 NLP does the same, step by step.

---

## 🔹 Summary

* NLP tasks are **basic operations on text**
* Common tasks include:

  * Tokenization
  * POS tagging
  * Named Entity Recognition
  * Text classification
  * Sentiment analysis
* These tasks are building blocks for advanced systems
