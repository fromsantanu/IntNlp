# 📘 Chapter 1.3 — Basic Text Processing

---

Before a computer can understand language, it must first **clean and prepare the text**.

👉 This step is called **Text Processing (or Text Preprocessing)**
👉 It is the **foundation of all NLP tasks**

Think of it like preparing vegetables before cooking:

* Wash → Clean
* Cut → Break into pieces
* Remove waste → Keep only useful parts

Similarly, in NLP, we prepare text before analysis.

---

## 🔹 Why Do We Need Text Processing?

Raw text is messy:

* Mixed uppercase/lowercase
* Punctuation marks
* Extra spaces
* Irrelevant words

### Example:

> “I LOVE this movie!!! 😍”

👉 For humans → easy
👉 For machines → confusing

So we convert it into a cleaner form:

> “i love this movie”

---

## 🔹 Basic Steps in Text Processing

Let’s go step by step using a simple sentence:

> “I am loving this movie!!!”

---

## 🔸 1. Lowercasing

Convert all text into lowercase.

### Example:

* “I LOVE NLP” → “i love nlp”

👉 Why?

* “Love” and “love” should be treated the same

---

## 🔸 2. Removing Punctuation

Remove symbols like:

* ! , . ? ; :

### Example:

* “movie!!!” → “movie”

👉 Helps reduce noise in data

---

---

## 🔸 4. Removing Stop Words

Stop words are very common words that don’t add much meaning.

### Examples:

* “is”, “am”, “the”, “and”, “a”

### Example:

* “I am loving this movie”
  → “loving movie”

👉 Focus on important words only

---

## 🔸 5. Stemming

Stemming reduces words to their **root form**.

![Image](https://images.openai.com/static-rsc-4/wekOj3rUwDN11J7m7z_QWBhkLonvzQjXjVYlIbC5oZujf78frcLKei-hh-vPWghBoeB3pAwH-GKGm-rXYCBzNvt5mhHMPOb87HALB3H8hSAfsfHfUWwLmOlIEpn5wVEPoI5qxgo1ek0AVhvwdURcgcoNJCxLnSHAJEilFE1ET4mnAAW58h3KUU3BBJqw7qaw?purpose=fullsize)

### Example:

* “running”, “runs”, “runner”
  → “run”

👉 Simple but sometimes not perfectly correct

---

## 🔸 6. Lemmatization

Lemmatization is similar to stemming but **more accurate**.

![Image](https://images.openai.com/static-rsc-4/38bpqS_CksOS_LVafKUXcsxbOa8MiywpHn_wsIk5Egaapo_G9bNIjTGaiHiWdISGy-AAVtZLQrQgXgz6H-jajZdwlUpPA5n3wsznW3PrjPAHXaOYyiF8mTn8gyODaqAb4pi_8_Igbb7v0Jps53A58CcrCPNCPt17m9evq1TTFW_LwnYQ9lUTT0X4CjAztNJo?purpose=fullsize)

### Example:

* “better” → “good”
* “running” → “run”

👉 Uses dictionary and grammar rules

---

## 🔸 7. Removing Numbers and Special Characters

### Example:

* “Price is ₹500!!!”
  → “price is”

👉 Helps when numbers are not useful

---

## 🔸 8. Handling Whitespace

Remove extra spaces:

### Example:

* “I    love   NLP” → “I love NLP”

---

## 🔹 Putting It All Together

### Original Text:

> “I am LOVING this movie!!! 😍”

### After Processing:

1. Lowercase → “i am loving this movie!!!”
2. Remove punctuation → “i am loving this movie”
3. Tokenize → [“i”, “am”, “loving”, “this”, “movie”]
4. Remove stop words → [“loving”, “movie”]
5. Lemmatize → [“love”, “movie”]

👉 Final Output:

> “love movie”

---

## 🔹 A Simple Real-Life Analogy

Think of reading a messy handwritten note:

* You ignore extra marks
* Focus on important words
* Understand the core message

👉 That’s exactly what text preprocessing does for machines.

---

## 🔹 Common Tools for Text Processing

In practice, we use libraries like:

* Python’s **NLTK**
* **spaCy**
* **TextBlob**

These tools provide ready-made functions for:

* Tokenization
* Stop word removal
* Lemmatization

---

## 🔹 Why This Step is Important

If preprocessing is poor:

* Model gets confused
* Accuracy drops

If preprocessing is good:

* Model performs better
* Faster and more efficient

👉 Garbage in → Garbage out
👉 Clean input → Better results

---

## 🔹 Summary

* Text processing is the **first step in NLP**
* It cleans and prepares raw text
* Key steps include:

  * Lowercasing
  * Tokenization
  * Stop word removal
  * Stemming / Lemmatization
* It improves the performance of NLP models
