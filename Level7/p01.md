# 📘 Chapter 7.1 — Probability in NLP

---

In earlier chapters, we saw that NLP models often:

* Predict the next word
* Classify text
* Rank possible outputs

👉 All of this is based on one key idea:

# 🔹 Probability

---

## 🔹 What is Probability?

👉 Probability tells us:

**How likely something is to happen**

---

### 🔹 Simple Example

* Toss a coin

  * Head → 50%
  * Tail → 50%

👉 Probability helps us measure **uncertainty**

---

## 🔹 Why Probability is Important in NLP

Language is **not fixed**—it is uncertain.

---

### Example:

> “I am going to the ____”

👉 Possible words:

* market (0.5)
* school (0.3)
* park (0.2)

👉 NLP models choose the word with **highest probability**

---

## 🔹 Real-Life Analogy

Think of guessing:

* If someone says “I am going to sleep”
  👉 You expect “sleep” more than “dance”

👉 That expectation = probability

---

# 🔸 1. Basic Probability Concepts

---

## 🔹 1.1 Probability of an Event

$P(A) = \frac{\text{Number of favorable outcomes}}{\text{Total outcomes}}$

---

### Example:

* Dice roll
* Probability of getting 3 = 1/6

---

## 🔹 1.2 Joint Probability

👉 Probability of multiple events happening together

---

### Example:

* “I” followed by “love”


$P(I, love)$


---

## 🔹 1.3 Conditional Probability

👉 Probability of one event given another

---

$P(A|B) = \frac{P(A, B)}{P(B)}$

---

### Example:

* Probability of “love” given “I”

👉 $( P(love \mid I) )$

---

## 🔹 Why Important in NLP?

👉 Language depends on context

* “I” → likely followed by “am”, “love”, etc.

---

# 🔸 2. Chain Rule in Language

---

👉 Used to calculate probability of a sentence

---

$P(w_1, w_2, ..., w_n) = P(w_1) \cdot P(w_2|w_1) \cdot P(w_3|w_1, w_2) \cdots$

---

### Example:

> “I love NLP”

👉 Probability:

* $P(I)$
* $P(love | I)$
* $P(NLP | I, love)$

---

👉 This is the foundation of **language models**

---

# 🔸 3. Independence Assumption

---

👉 To simplify calculations:

We assume words depend only on a few previous words

---

### Example (Bigram):


$P(w_3 | w_1, w_2) \approx P(w_3 | w_2)$


---

👉 This reduces complexity

---

# 🔸 4. Probability in Classification

---

## 🔹 Example: Spam Detection

Message:

> “Win money now”

👉 Model calculates:

* $P(Spam | message)$
* $P(Not Spam | message)$

---

👉 Choose higher probability

---

## 🔹 Used in:

* Naive Bayes
* Logistic Regression

# 🔸 7. Log Probability

---

👉 Used to handle very small numbers

---

### Why?

Multiplying many small probabilities → becomes extremely small

👉 Use log to simplify

---

## 🔹 Example

Instead of:

* 0.001 × 0.002

👉 Use:

* $log(0.001) + log(0.002)$
