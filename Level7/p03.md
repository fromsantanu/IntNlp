# 🔹 Information Theory

---

## 🔹 What is Information Theory?

👉 Information theory studies:

**How much information is present in data**

👉 In NLP, it helps us measure:

* Uncertainty
* Predictability
* Model performance

---

## 🔹 Real-Life Analogy

Think of guessing words:

* If something is obvious → less information
* If something is surprising → more information

👉 Information = surprise

---

## 🔹 Example

Sentence:

> “The sun rises in the ____”

👉 Expected → “east”

👉 Low surprise → low information

---

Sentence:

> “The sun rises in the banana”

👉 Unexpected → high surprise → high information

---

# 🔸 1. Entropy

---

## 🔹 What is Entropy?

👉 Entropy measures **uncertainty in a system**

---

## 🔹 Formula

H(X) = - \sum P(x) \log P(x)

---

## 🔹 Simple Meaning

* High entropy → high uncertainty
* Low entropy → low uncertainty

---

## 🔹 Example

---

### Case 1: Equal Probability

| Word | Probability |
| ---- | ----------- |
| cat  | 0.5         |
| dog  | 0.5         |

👉 High uncertainty → high entropy

---

### Case 2: Unequal Probability

| Word | Probability |
| ---- | ----------- |
| cat  | 0.9         |
| dog  | 0.1         |

👉 Low uncertainty → low entropy

---

## 🔹 Intuition

* If all options are equally likely → hard to predict
* If one option dominates → easy to predict

# 🔸 2. Cross-Entropy

---

## 🔹 What is Cross-Entropy?

👉 Measures difference between:

* Actual distribution
* Predicted distribution

---

## 🔹 Simple Idea

👉 If prediction is close to truth → low loss
👉 If prediction is wrong → high loss

---

## 🔹 Used in NLP

* Classification tasks
* Language models

---

# 🔸 3. Perplexity

---

## 🔹 What is Perplexity?

👉 Perplexity measures how well a language model predicts text

---

## 🔹 Formula

Perplexity = 2^{H(X)}

---

## 🔹 Simple Meaning

👉 Perplexity = “confusion level” of the model

---

### Interpretation:

* Low perplexity → good model ✅
* High perplexity → poor model ❌

---

## 🔹 Example

Sentence:

> “I love NLP”

---

### Model A:

* Predicts correctly → low perplexity

---

### Model B:

* Random guesses → high perplexity

---

## 🔹 Intuition

Think of guessing:

* If you have 2 choices → easy → low perplexity
* If you have 100 choices → hard → high perplexity

---

# 🔸 4. Entropy vs Perplexity

| Concept    | Meaning             |
| ---------- | ------------------- |
| Entropy    | Uncertainty in data |
| Perplexity | Model’s confusion   |

---

👉 Relationship:

* Higher entropy → higher perplexity
* Lower entropy → lower perplexity

---

# 🔸 5. Why These Concepts Matter in NLP

---

## 🔹 Used in:

* Language modeling
* Model evaluation
* Training loss functions

---

## 🔹 Example Applications:

* GPT → minimizes cross-entropy
* N-gram models → evaluated using perplexity

---

# 🔹 Real-Life Analogy (Revisited)

Think of a student guessing answers:

* If student knows topic → low confusion → low perplexity
* If guessing randomly → high confusion → high perplexity

---

## 🔹 Key Insights

* Entropy measures uncertainty
* Cross-entropy measures prediction error
* Perplexity measures model performance

---

## 🔹 Summary

* Information theory helps measure uncertainty in language
* Key concepts:

  * Entropy → uncertainty
  * Cross-entropy → prediction error
  * Perplexity → model confusion
* Important for evaluating language models
