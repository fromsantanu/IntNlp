# 📘 Chapter 7.5 — Graph-based NLP & Knowledge Graphs

---

So far, we have studied NLP mainly as:

* Sequences of words
* Vectors and embeddings

👉 But language is not just sequences—it is also about **relationships**.

👉 This leads to an important concept:

# 🔹 Graph-based NLP & Knowledge Graphs

---

## 🔹 What is a Graph?

👉 A graph is a structure made of:

* **Nodes (entities)**
* **Edges (relationships)**

---

### 🔹 Simple Example

* Node → “Ravi”
* Node → “Kolkata”
* Edge → “lives in”

👉 Graph:

> Ravi → lives in → Kolkata

---

## 🔹 Real-Life Analogy

Think of a social network:

* People → nodes
* Friendships → edges

👉 NLP graphs work similarly

---

# 🔸 1. Graph-based NLP

---

## 🔹 What is Graph-based NLP?

👉 Represent text as a graph instead of a sequence

---

## 🔹 Example Sentence

> “Ravi works at Google”

---

### Graph Representation:

* Ravi → Person
* Google → Organization
* Edge → works at

---

## 🔹 Why Use Graphs?

* Capture relationships
* Better understanding of structure
* Useful for complex reasoning

---

# 🔸 2. Knowledge Graphs

---

## 🔹 What is a Knowledge Graph?

👉 A structured representation of real-world knowledge using graphs

---

### Format:

**(Entity — Relationship — Entity)**

---

### Example:

* (Ravi — lives in — Kolkata)
* (Kolkata — located in — India)

---

👉 This creates a connected network of knowledge

---

## 🔹 Where Knowledge Graphs are Used

* Google Search
* Recommendation systems
* Question answering
* Chatbots

---

## 🔹 Example Use Case

Query:

> “Where does Ravi live?”

👉 Graph traversal:

* Ravi → lives in → Kolkata

👉 Answer:

> “Kolkata”

# 🔸 3. Building Knowledge Graphs

---

## 🔹 Steps

---

### Step 1: Entity Extraction

* Identify entities (NER)

---

### Step 2: Relationship Extraction

* Identify connections

---

### Step 3: Graph Construction

* Create nodes and edges

---

### Step 4: Storage

* Store in graph database

---

* RDF (Resource Description Framework)

# 🔸 4. Graph Neural Networks (GNNs)

---

## 🔹 What are GNNs?

👉 Neural networks designed for graph data

---

## 🔹 Idea

👉 Nodes learn from neighbors

---

### Example:

* “Ravi” connected to “Kolkata”
  👉 Learns location information

---

## 🔹 Why Important?

* Capture complex relationships
* Useful for reasoning

* Captures relationships clearly
* Supports reasoning

---

# 🔹 Limitations

* Requires structured data
* Scaling can be difficult

---

## 🔹 Graph NLP vs Traditional NLP

| Feature        | Traditional NLP | Graph NLP             |
| -------------- | --------------- | --------------------- |
| Representation | Sequence        | Graph                 |
| Focus          | Words           | Relationships         |
| Strength       | Simplicity      | Structure & reasoning |
