---

So far, we have learned:

* Basic NLP tasks (tokenization, classification)
* Machine learning and embeddings
* Sequence models (RNN, LSTM, GRU)

👉 Now we move to **Advanced NLP Tasks**
👉 These tasks help machines understand **language structure deeply**

---

## 🔹 What are Advanced NLP Tasks?

👉 These are tasks where the model doesn’t just read text, but tries to:

* Understand grammar
* Identify important entities
* Analyze sentence structure

👉 Think of this as moving from:

* “reading words” → “understanding language”

---

## 🔹 Overview of Topics

We will cover:

1. Named Entity Recognition (NER)
2. Part-of-Speech (POS) Tagging
3. Parsing (Sentence Structure Analysis)

---

# 🔸 1. Named Entity Recognition (NER)

## 🔹 What is NER?

NER identifies **important real-world entities** in text.

---

## 🔹 Types of Entities

* Person
* Location
* Organization
* Date / Time
* Money

---

## 🔹 Example

Sentence:

> “Ravi works at Google in Kolkata”

---

### Output:

| Word    | Entity Type  |
| ------- | ------------ |
| Ravi    | Person       |
| Google  | Organization |
| Kolkata | Location     |

# 🔸 2. Part-of-Speech (POS) Tagging

## 🔹 What is POS Tagging?

Assigning **grammatical roles** to each word.

---

## 🔹 Common Tags

* Noun (NN)
* Verb (VB)
* Adjective (JJ)
* Adverb (RB)

---

## 🔹 Example

Sentence:

> “The boy runs fast”

---

### Output:

| Word | POS        |
| ---- | ---------- |
| The  | Determiner |
| boy  | Noun       |
| runs | Verb       |
| fast | Adverb     |

---

## 🔹 Why Important?

# 🔸 3. Parsing (Sentence Structure Analysis)

## 🔹 What is Parsing?

Parsing analyzes the **structure of a sentence**

👉 It shows how words are connected

---

## 🔹 Types of Parsing

---

### 🔸 1. Constituency Parsing

👉 Breaks sentence into phrases

---

### Example:

> “The boy eats an apple”

👉 Structure:

* [The boy] → noun phrase
* [eats an apple] → verb phrase

---

---

### 🔸 2. Dependency Parsing

👉 Shows relationships between words

---

### Example:

* “boy” → subject of “eats”
* “apple” → object of “eats”

---

## 🔹 Why Parsing is Important

* Helps understand meaning deeply
* Used in:

  * Machine translation
  * Question answering
  * Grammar checking

---

## 🔹 Real-Life Analogy

Think of a sentence like a family:

* POS tagging → identifies roles (father, mother, child)
* NER → identifies names (Ravi, Kolkata)
* Parsing → shows relationships (who is connected to whom)

---

## 🔹 How These Tasks Work Together

Sentence:

> “Ravi is playing cricket in Kolkata”

---

### Combined Analysis:

* NER → Ravi (Person), Kolkata (Location)
* POS → Ravi (Noun), playing (Verb)
* Parsing → Ravi → subject of playing

👉 Together → full understanding

---

## 🔹 Models Used

These tasks are solved using:

* Hidden Markov Models (HMM)
* Conditional Random Fields (CRF)
* RNN / LSTM / GRU
* Transformers (modern approach)

---

## 🔹 Challenges in Advanced NLP

* Ambiguity
* Complex sentence structures
* Long dependencies
* Multiple meanings

---

## 🔹 Summary

* Advanced NLP tasks help in **deep language understanding**
* Key tasks:

  * NER → identifies entities
  * POS tagging → identifies grammar
  * Parsing → identifies structure
* These tasks work together to analyze text fully
