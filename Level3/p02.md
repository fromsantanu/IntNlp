# 📘 Chapter 3.2 — Sequence Models (RNN, LSTM, GRU)

---

In previous chapters, we learned:

* Word embeddings represent meaning
* Machine learning models use features

👉 But there is still a problem:

**Language is sequential (ordered)**
👉 The meaning depends on the order of words

---

## 🔹 Why Sequence Models are Needed

### Example:

* “I love you” ❤️
* “You love I” ❌

👉 Same words, different meaning

👉 Traditional ML models (like BoW) **ignore order**
👉 Sequence models solve this by **processing words one by one**

---

## 🔹 What are Sequence Models?

👉 Models that process data **in sequence (step by step)**

* Input: sequence of words
* Output: prediction based on sequence

---

## 🔹 Real-Life Analogy

Think of reading a sentence:

* You read word by word
* You remember previous words
* You understand meaning gradually

👉 Sequence models work the same way

---

# 🔸 1. Recurrent Neural Network (RNN)

## 🔹 Basic Idea

👉 RNN processes one word at a time and **remembers previous information**

---

## 🔹 How it Works

* Input word → processed
* Hidden state → stores memory
* Passed to next step

---

## 🔹 Example

Sentence:

> “I love NLP”

Steps:

1. Read “I” → store info
2. Read “love” → combine with previous
3. Read “NLP” → final understanding

---

## 🔹 Problem with RNN

👉 **Vanishing Gradient Problem**

* Long sentences → early words forgotten
* Memory becomes weak over time

---

## 🔹 Example Problem

> “I grew up in a village near a river… (long sentence)… I love that place”

👉 RNN may forget “village” by the time it reaches “place”

---

# 🔸 2. Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM)

## 🔹 Why LSTM?

👉 Designed to **solve RNN memory problem**

---

## 🔹 Key Idea

👉 LSTM has a **memory cell** that can:

* Keep important information
* Forget unnecessary information

---

## 🔹 Components (Simple View)

* Forget Gate → what to remove
* Input Gate → what to store
* Output Gate → what to use

---

## 🔹 Visualization

![Image](https://images.openai.com/static-rsc-4/RieXTeKSmIVvK6B40LSWOdquVVkaH90rAbu_hmPTxP--g88SFNJEWW0AS1ikYRxrTLTjt658PnSoWUfcjG71vrwM6_IIOyU7GAz4me0EEX_3wrYl9E4cng_wMLIXbxDFY_zu5dc-Ic42TaNjexHYLv-4eQs_0UALY_WJc254i1-7NM-R0DEgCoAj5g2AZHWr?purpose=fullsize)

---

## 🔹 Example

Sentence:

> “The movie was not good”

👉 LSTM remembers:

* “not” → important
  👉 So final meaning = negative

---

## 🔹 Advantage

* Handles long sentences better
* Keeps important context

---

# 🔸 3. Gated Recurrent Unit (GRU)

## 🔹 Idea

👉 Simplified version of LSTM

---

## 🔹 Key Features

* Fewer gates than LSTM
* Faster to train
* Similar performance

---

## 🔹 Components

* Update Gate
* Reset Gate

---

## 🔹 Visualization

## 🔹 Advantage

* Faster than LSTM
* Works well in many tasks

---

# 🔹 Comparison of Models

| Feature     | RNN                | LSTM    | GRU              |
| ----------- | ------------------ | ------- | ---------------- |
| Memory      | Short              | Long    | Medium/Long      |
| Complexity  | Simple             | Complex | Medium           |
| Speed       | Fast               | Slow    | Faster than LSTM |
| Performance | Weak for long text | Strong  | Strong           |

---

## 🔹 Where Sequence Models are Used

* Machine Translation
* Speech Recognition
* Text Generation
* Chatbots

---

## 🔹 Limitations of Sequence Models

* Slow (process word by word)
* Difficult for very long text
* Training can be complex

👉 These limitations led to **Transformers (modern models)**

---

## 🔹 Real-Life Analogy

Think of remembering a story:

* RNN → forgets early parts
* LSTM → remembers key events
* GRU → remembers efficiently with less effort

---

## 🔹 Summary

* Language is sequential → order matters
* RNN processes sequences step by step
* LSTM improves memory using gates
* GRU simplifies LSTM with similar performance
* These models are the foundation of deep learning in NLP
