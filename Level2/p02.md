# 📘 Chapter 2.2 — Text Representation (BoW, N-grams, TF-IDF)

---

After preprocessing, we get clean text like:

> “love movie”

👉 But computers still **cannot understand words directly**
👉 Machines only understand **numbers**

So we need to convert text into numbers.
This process is called **Text Representation**.

---

## 🔹 Why Text Representation is Needed

Think of a computer like a calculator:

* It understands numbers (1, 2, 3)
* It does **not understand words** (“love”, “movie”)

👉 So we convert text into numeric form so that:

* Machine Learning models can process it
* Patterns can be learned

---

## 🔹 Overview of Methods

In this chapter, we will learn:

1. Bag of Words (BoW)
2. N-grams
3. TF-IDF

---

# 🔸 1. Bag of Words (BoW)

## 🔹 Idea

BoW represents text by **counting how many times each word appears**.

👉 It ignores:

* Grammar
* Word order

👉 Focus: only word frequency

---

## 🔹 Example

### Sentences:

1. “I love NLP”
2. “I love machine learning”

---

### Step 1: Create Vocabulary

All unique words:

> [I, love, NLP, machine, learning]

---

### Step 2: Count Words

| Sentence | I | love | NLP | machine | learning |
| -------- | - | ---- | --- | ------- | -------- |
| S1       | 1 | 1    | 1   | 0       | 0        |
| S2       | 1 | 1    | 0   | 1       | 1        |

👉 Each sentence becomes a **vector of numbers**

---

## 🔹 Intuition

Think of it like counting items in a shopping bag:

* 2 apples
* 1 banana

👉 You ignore order, just count items

---

## 🔹 Advantages

* Simple
* Easy to implement

## 🔹 Limitations

* Ignores word order
* Treats all words equally

---

# 🔸 2. N-grams

## 🔹 Idea

N-grams consider **sequence of words**.

👉 Instead of single words, we take groups of words

---

## 🔹 Types

* Unigram → single word
* Bigram → 2 words
* Trigram → 3 words

---

## 🔹 Example

Sentence:

> “I love NLP”

![Image](https://images.openai.com/static-rsc-4/Us9W0a-6KETYKLoRdJFmijiuj3w8uUM8poXsI3g1qnWN8bS6rGJz0XmFMLZSvdnY9xiAG563L07fK5cwR2POrlLWoCIU4GP_a7pNkbwMixKDfM99tr-uZQZtLUbtmWrvvPNimmdTR1tvnCkDjnUNc9A8FuSJJeEEDponkgLkusnBplOTM40X94EkybeGL5Cc?purpose=fullsize)

### Output:

* Unigram → [I, love, NLP]
* Bigram → [I love, love NLP]
* Trigram → [I love NLP]

---

## 🔹 Why Useful?

BoW ignores order, but N-grams capture it.

### Example:

* “not good” vs “good”

👉 Bigram helps preserve meaning

---

## 🔹 Advantages

* Captures context
* Better than simple BoW

## 🔹 Limitations

* Vocabulary becomes very large
* More computation needed

---

# 🔸 3. TF-IDF (Term Frequency – Inverse Document Frequency)

## 🔹 Problem with BoW

BoW treats all words equally.

👉 But some words are not important:

* “the”, “is”, “and”

👉 TF-IDF solves this problem.

---

## 🔹 Idea

TF-IDF gives **higher weight to important words**
and **lower weight to common words**

---

## 🔹 Formula

TF\text{-}IDF = TF \times IDF

---

### 🔸 Term Frequency (TF)

How often a word appears in a document

[
TF = \frac{\text{word count}}{\text{total words}}
]

---

### 🔸 Inverse Document Frequency (IDF)

How rare a word is across documents

[
IDF = \log\left(\frac{\text{total documents}}{\text{documents containing word}}\right)
]

---

## 🔹 Example

### Sentences:

1. “I love NLP”
2. “I love AI”

👉 Word “love” appears in both → less important
👉 Word “NLP” appears once → more important

👉 TF-IDF gives:

* Lower weight to “love”
* Higher weight to “NLP”

---

## 🔹 Intuition

Think of a classroom:

* Common words → like common names (less unique)
* Rare words → like unique names (more important)

---

## 🔹 Advantages

* Highlights important words
* Reduces noise

## 🔹 Limitations

* Still ignores deep meaning
* Cannot capture context like modern models

---

## 🔹 Comparison of Methods

| Feature           | BoW    | N-grams   | TF-IDF    |
| ----------------- | ------ | --------- | --------- |
| Word Order        | ❌ No   | ✅ Yes     | ❌ No      |
| Simplicity        | ✅ Easy | ⚠️ Medium | ⚠️ Medium |
| Importance Weight | ❌ No   | ❌ No      | ✅ Yes     |
| Context           | ❌ No   | ✅ Partial | ❌ No      |

---

## 🔹 Real-Life Analogy

Think of reading books:

* BoW → counting how many times each word appears
* N-grams → reading phrases instead of single words
* TF-IDF → focusing on rare, important words

---

## 🔹 Summary

* Computers need text in **numeric form**
* BoW → counts words
* N-grams → captures word sequences
* TF-IDF → gives importance to meaningful words

👉 These are **foundation techniques** before advanced models like word embeddings.
