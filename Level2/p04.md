# 🔹 What is a Language Model?

A **Language Model (LM)** is a system that can:

👉 **predict the next word in a sentence**

---

## 🔹 Simple Idea

### Example:

* “I am going to the ____”

👉 Possible predictions:

* market
* school
* park

👉 A language model assigns **probabilities**:

* P(market) = 0.4
* P(school) = 0.3
* P(park) = 0.3

👉 It chooses the most likely word

---

## 🔹 Why Language Models are Important

Language models are used in:

* Chatbots (like ChatGPT)
* Machine Translation
* Text Generation
* Speech Recognition

👉 Basically, any system that **understands or generates text**

---

## 🔹 How Language Models Work

Language models use **probability of word sequences**

---

### 🔸 Example Sentence:

> “I love NLP”

👉 Instead of treating words separately, we calculate:

* Probability of full sentence
* Based on sequence of words

---

## 🔹 Chain Rule (Basic Idea)

A sentence probability is broken into smaller parts:

$P(w_1, w_2, ..., w_n) = P(w_1) \cdot P(w_2|w_1) \cdot P(w_3|w_1, w_2) \cdots P(w_n|w_1,...,w_{n-1})$

👉 Meaning:

* Each word depends on previous words

---

## 🔹 N-gram Language Models

To simplify the above complex formula, we use **N-grams**

👉 We assume each word depends only on a few previous words

---

## 🔸 Types:

### 1. Unigram Model

* Each word independent

👉 Example:

* “I love NLP”
  → $P(I) × P(love) × P(NLP)$

❌ Problem: ignores context

---

### 2. Bigram Model

* Each word depends on previous word

👉 Example:

* $P(love | I)$
* $P(NLP | love)$

---

### 3. Trigram Model

* Depends on last two words

👉 Example:

* $P(NLP | I love)$

## 🔹 Example Calculation (Bigram)

Sentence:

> “I love NLP”

👉 Probability:

* $P(I) × P(love | I) × P(NLP | love)$

👉 If:

* $P(love | I) is high → sentence is more natural$

---

## 🔹 Problem: Data Sparsity

Sometimes combinations don’t appear in training data.

### Example:

* “I love deep learning”

👉 Maybe “deep learning” not seen before

👉 So probability becomes zero ❌

---

## 🔹 Solution: Smoothing

Smoothing adjusts probabilities to avoid zero.

### Example:

* Assign small probability to unseen words

👉 Common techniques:

* Laplace Smoothing
* Add-k smoothing

---

## 🔹 Perplexity (Basic Idea)

👉 Used to evaluate language models

👉 Measures how “confused” the model is

* Low perplexity → good model
* High perplexity → poor model

---

## 🔹 Real-Life Analogy

Think of typing on your phone:

* You type: “I am going to”
* Phone suggests: “sleep”, “school”, “home”

👉 That suggestion system is a **language model**

---

## 🔹 Limitations of Basic Language Models

* Cannot handle long context well
* Data sparsity problem
* Memory limitations

👉 Example:

* Long sentences lose meaning in N-gram models

---

## 🔹 Evolution Beyond Basics

Basic language models led to:

* Neural Language Models
* Word Embeddings
* Transformers (modern models)

👉 These solve many limitations of N-grams

---

## 🔹 Summary

* Language models predict **next word in sequence**
* Use probability of word sequences
* N-grams simplify computation:

  * Unigram
  * Bigram
  * Trigram
* Smoothing handles missing data
* Perplexity evaluates model performance
