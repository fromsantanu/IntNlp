So far, we have learned:

* How text is processed and represented

# 🔹 Information Retrieval (IR)

---

## 🔹 What is an Information Retrieval System?

👉 An Information Retrieval System is used to:

**Find relevant information from a large collection of data**

---

### 🔹 Simple Example

You type in Google:

> “best books on NLP”

👉 Google returns:

* Relevant web pages
* Articles
* Books

👉 This is an **Information Retrieval System**

---

## 🔹 Real-Life Analogy

Think of a library:

* You ask the librarian for a book
* The librarian finds the most relevant books

👉 IR system = digital librarian

---

## 🔹 Key Components of an IR System

---

## 🔸 1. Document Collection

👉 A large set of documents

### Example:

* Web pages
* Articles
* Books

---

## 🔸 2. Query

👉 The user’s request

### Example:

* “machine learning basics”

---

## 🔸 3. Indexing

👉 Organizing documents for fast search

---

## 🔸 4. Retrieval

👉 Finding relevant documents

---

## 🔸 5. Ranking

👉 Ordering results by relevance

---

## 🔹 How an IR System Works (Step-by-Step)

---

### Step 1: Collect Documents

* Store large text data

---

### Step 2: Preprocess Text

* Tokenization
* Stop word removal
* Normalization

---

### Step 3: Create Index

👉 Instead of searching every document:

👉 Create an **inverted index**

---

### 🔸 Example of Inverted Index:

| Word     | Documents |
| -------- | --------- |
| NLP      | D1, D3    |
| learning | D1, D2    |

👉 Faster searching

---

### Step 4: Process Query

User input:

> “NLP learning”

---

### Step 5: Retrieve Documents

* Find documents containing query words

---

### Step 6: Rank Results

👉 Use scoring methods to rank relevance

---

## 🔹 Retrieval Models

---

## 🔸 1. Boolean Model

👉 Uses simple logic:

* AND
* OR
* NOT

---

### Example:

* “NLP AND learning”

👉 Returns documents containing both

---

## 🔸 2. Vector Space Model

👉 Represents documents as vectors

👉 Uses similarity (like cosine similarity)

---

### Example:

* Compare query vector with document vectors
* Higher similarity → higher rank

---

## 🔸 3. Probabilistic Model

👉 Uses probability to estimate relevance

👉 Example:

* BM25 (popular method)

---

## 🔹 Ranking Methods

---

### 🔸 TF-IDF

👉 Measures importance of words

---

### 🔸 Cosine Similarity

👉 Measures similarity between vectors

---

## 🔹 Evaluation of IR Systems

---

### 🔸 Precision

👉 How many retrieved documents are relevant

---

### 🔸 Recall

👉 How many relevant documents are retrieved

---

### 🔸 F1 Score

👉 Balance between precision and recall

---

## 🔹 Applications of IR Systems

* Search engines (Google, Bing)
* Digital libraries
* E-commerce search
* Question answering systems

---

## 🔹 Challenges in IR

* Ambiguous queries
* Synonyms
* Large-scale data
* Ranking accuracy

---

## 🔹 Modern IR (Neural IR)

👉 Uses deep learning and embeddings

---

### Example:

* Instead of keyword matching
* Understands meaning

---

👉 “car” ≈ “automobile”

---

## 🔹 Real-Life Analogy (Revisited)

Think of searching for a book:

* Boolean → strict rules
* Vector model → similarity
* Neural IR → understands meaning

---

## 🔹 Summary

* IR systems retrieve relevant information from large data
* Key steps:

  * Indexing
  * Query processing
  * Retrieval
  * Ranking
* Models:

  * Boolean
  * Vector Space
  * Probabilistic
* Modern systems use embeddings and deep learning
