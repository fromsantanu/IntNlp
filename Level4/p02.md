In the previous chapter, we learned about **Transformers**.

👉 Now we explore one of the most powerful ideas in modern NLP:

# 🔹 Pretrained Language Models

---

## 🔹 What are Pretrained Language Models?

👉 These are models that are:

1. **Trained on huge amounts of text data**
2. Then reused for specific tasks

---

### 🔹 Simple Idea

Think of a student:

* First studies general knowledge (school)
* Then specializes (college)

👉 Similarly:

* Pretraining → general language learning
* Fine-tuning → specific task

---

## 🔹 Why Pretraining is Important

Without pretraining:

* Need huge data for every task ❌
* Training is slow ❌

With pretraining:

* Already understands language basics ✅
* Needs less data for new tasks ✅

---

## 🔹 Two Main Steps

---

### 🔸 1. Pretraining

👉 Learn general language patterns from large text

---

### 🔸 2. Fine-Tuning

👉 Adapt model for a specific task

---

### Example:

* Pretrained model → understands English
* Fine-tuned → sentiment analysis

---

## 🔹 Types of Pretrained Models

We will study:

1. BERT
2. GPT
3. T5

---

# 🔸 1. BERT (Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers)

---

## 🔹 Key Idea

👉 BERT reads text **in both directions**

* Left → Right
* Right → Left

👉 This helps understand context better

---

## 🔹 Example

Sentence:

> “The bank is near the river”

👉 BERT understands:

* “bank” = river bank (based on full context)

---

## 🔹 Training Method

👉 Masked Language Modeling (MLM)

---

### Example:

* “I love [MASK]”

👉 Model predicts:

* NLP
* food
* coding

# 🔸 2. GPT (Generative Pretrained Transformer)

---

## 🔹 Key Idea

👉 GPT generates text **word by word (left to right)**

---

## 🔹 Example

Input:

> “Once upon a time…”

👉 GPT continues:

* “there was a king…”

---

## 🔹 Training Method

👉 Next Word Prediction

---

### Example:

* “I love” → predict next word

---

# 🔸 3. T5 (Text-To-Text Transfer Transformer)

---

## 🔹 Key Idea

👉 Treat **every NLP task as text-to-text**

---

## 🔹 Example

| Task               | Input                               | Output          |
| ------------------ | ----------------------------------- | --------------- |
| Translation        | “Translate English to Hindi: Hello” | “नमस्ते”        |
| Summarization      | “Summarize: long text…”             | “short summary” |
| Question Answering | “Question: … Context: …”            | “Answer”        |

---

## 🔹 Strength

* One model for multiple tasks
* Very flexible

---

# 🔹 Comparison of Models

| Feature      | BERT          | GPT          | T5                     |
| ------------ | ------------- | ------------ | ---------------------- |
| Direction    | Bidirectional | Left → Right | Both (Encoder-Decoder) |
| Purpose      | Understanding | Generation   | Both                   |
| Training     | Masked words  | Next word    | Text-to-text           |
| Architecture | Encoder       | Decoder      | Encoder + Decoder      |

---

## 🔹 Real-Life Analogy

Think of three types of students:

* BERT → understands questions deeply
* GPT → writes answers creatively
* T5 → can do both and convert tasks into simple format

---

## 🔹 Why These Models are Powerful

* Trained on massive data
* Capture deep language patterns
* Work well across many tasks

---

## 🔹 Limitations

* Require large computational resources
* May generate incorrect or biased outputs
* Hard to interpret internally

---

## 🔹 Summary

* Pretrained models learn general language first
* Then fine-tuned for specific tasks
* Key models:

  * BERT → understanding
  * GPT → generation
  * T5 → unified text-to-text
* These models power modern NLP systems
